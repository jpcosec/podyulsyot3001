# Selected Screenshots

This is the curated set of 5 screenshots that best explains the current product and the intended review flow.

## 1. `01-job-workspace-overview.png`

- Why it matters:
  - shows the main per-job workspace
  - makes the pipeline/stage structure visible
  - establishes the product shell the operator works inside

## 2. `02-document-to-graph-review.png`

- Why it matters:
  - represents the extraction review step
  - shows source text linked to structured requirements
  - captures the core idea of grounded review

## 3. `03-document-editing.png`

- Why it matters:
  - represents the final drafting/review step
  - shows direct editing of generated documents from local artifacts
  - closes the operator loop from extraction to output refinement

## 4. `04-node-editor-sandbox.png`

- Why it matters:
  - shows the strongest current graph-editing interaction language in the repo
  - should inform the future HITL editing surface
  - important for a UI/UX designer because it contains the richest interaction vocabulary

## 5. `05-text-tagger-sandbox.png`

- Why it matters:
  - shows the annotation/tagging direction for text-grounded interaction
  - represents another important building block for evidence linking and review UX

## Narrative order

These 5 images tell the product story in this order:

1. open a job workspace
2. inspect source text and extracted structure
3. edit/refine generated output
4. understand the advanced graph interaction language already available
5. understand the text annotation direction that can support better evidence review
